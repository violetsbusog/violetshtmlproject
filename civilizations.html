Greek Civilization

⇒ Ancient Greek culture covers over a thousand years of history ⇐

✩ First advanced civilization of Europe known as the "Minoans" arose on the Greek Island of Crete.
↳ They however, slowly declined but still were able to influence those people on the Greek Mainland whom we know as the "Myceneans".
↳ They developed the earliest written form of Greek language.
↳ This was also the period when the mythic Greek heroes are said to have lived.


✩ Everything then violently fell to pieces in the lkate Bronze Age Collapse (1200 – 1150 BC)

• Dark ages (1100 – 800 BC)

↳ Collapse of the Mycenaean civilization
↳ Linear B script (the Mycenaean writing system), was also lost shortly after. In result, writing and advancement mostly stopped.

• Archaic Era (480 BC)

↳ A new light for the greeks where big things started to happen
↳ Greek city states started to form and these communities were called “polis”. (eg. Athens & Sparta)


✩ At this point Persia had the biggest empire history had ever known

↳They attacked Greece and the Greeks realized that if they stayed separate, not one city state would be able to defend themselves in order to survive, thus why they decided to unite all city states, form an alliance, and work together to defend Greece, which resulted in the defeat of the Persian invaders.


• Golden Age (495 – 429 BC)

↳ This was a time of prosperity for the Greeks.
↳ Athens, under the rule of Pericles, renewed the city after the invasion of the Persians.
↳ This era birthed our famous Greek philosophers, "the father of art, "the father of medicine", where art & architecture flourished, and so much more !


• Peloppenesian War (431 – 404 BC)

↳ Athens had attained great wealth and power, and the other Greek states were getting weary, especially Sparta.
↳ This war lasted for 27 years but left the Spartans victorious.
↳ Pericles soon passed away, leaving Athens with a series of horrible leaders...


• Rule of Alexander the Great (326 BC)

↳ He took over, ruled Greece, and conquered other areas which stretched from Egypt to India.
↳ He however, passed away as well at the age of 32 and since no one in Greece was strong enough to rule over such a large empire, it slowly broke up.


• Rome took over the Greek Empire (146 BC)

Roman Civilization

⇒ Rome was formed in the 8th century BC. It’s an amazing city in Italy which you can still visit today and what makes it so amazing is its rich history. ⇐

✩ Legend has it that Rome was founded by Remus and Romulus who were 2 brothers who were raised by a she wolf Romulus killed Remus and gave his name to the city, and became its first ruler.
✩ But many historians also believed that numerous farm communities in the 7 hills near Rome, joined together and formed the city.


⇒ But thats what legend says.... ⇐


✩ Rome was founded abt 2800 years ago with the union of several villages in Italy by the Tiber river with its neighbors being the Etrucians and Greeks.
↳ Rome was ruled by kings and in 509 BC, an arrogant and cruel king known as “Tarquin the Proud” became the last king of Rome.


• Republican Period

✩ Romans decied to prevent any such concentration of power ever again, decided to completely change Rome’s government, and made it into a republic which is a form of government where there are no kings but politicians who make the decisions which they think are convenient..
↳ 2 consuls were elected and they were ruled by the Senates/Patricians.
↳ The common people were known as the Plebians and even if they were as rich as the Plebians, they werent entitled to have a position in the state.
↳ They struggled for these rights for 200 years.
↳ They became the backbone of the roman army and to have their own way, they would threaten the state with immigration.
↳ Because of this struggle, Rome achieved the publication of the first written laws and by 287 BC, the Plebians received equal rights.

• Julius Ceasar

✩ He is one of the most famous people in Roman history and had a huge impact on ancient Rome.

• Imperial Period

↳ After the death of Julius Caesar, another civil war broke out.

↳ He was renamed Augustus and became the first emperor of Rome.
↳ He ushered a period called “Pax Romana” (Roman peace) where the people of the Roman empire were able to live their lives without the fear of invasion.
