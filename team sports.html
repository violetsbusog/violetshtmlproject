Team Sports

✩ There are a variety of them but, here are some of the Major Team Sports :

✓ Basketball

✩ How did it start?

↳ Basketball was created on December 1891 by YMCA gym teacher: James Naismith in Springfield Massachusettes.

✩ What is Basketball ?

↳ Basketball is a team sport in which two teams most commonly consisting of five players each try to score by shooting a ball through a hoop elevated 10 feet above the ground.

✩ How should you play ?

↳ The ball is moved down the court toward the basket by passing or dribbling. The team with the ball is called the "offense". The team without the ball is called the "defense". They compete with the primary objective of shooting a basketball through the defender's hoop to score, while preventing the opposing team from shooting through their own hoop.
↳ When a team makes a "basket", they score two points and the ball goes to the other team.

✩ Equipment and Facilities

↳ The game is played on a rectangular floor called the court, with a hoop also called the basket or net, at each end. A spherical ball is used with the standard size being 29.5 inches (75 cm) in circumference for men and a maximum circumference of 29 inches (74 cm) for women.


✓ Volleyball

✩ How did it start?

↳ It was invented by William G. Morgan on Feb. 9, 1895 as an instructor at the YMCA in Springfield Massachusettes where he met Jaimes Naismith.

✩ What is Volleyball ?

↳ Originally known as “mintonette,” it is a game played by two teams, usually of six players on a each side, in which the players use their hands to bat a ball back and forth over a high net, trying to make the ball touch the court within the opponents’ playing area before it can be returned.

✩ How should you play ?

↳ The six volleyball court positions are : setter, middle blocker, outside hitter, opposite hitter, libero and serving specialist.

↳ Then, the two teams will rally or hit the ball back and forth over the net until a fault occurs. There is a maximum of three hits per side. The objective is to score points by sending the ball over the net, grounding it into the opponent’s court. To win a game, a team must score 25 points with a two-point difference. If they’re too closely matched, the game can continue over the 25-point maximum.

✩ Equipment and Facilities

↳ The standard volleyball is made of leather or synthetic leather, weighs between 9 and 10 ounces and has a circumference of 25.6 to 26.4 inches. The court is 60 feet by 30 feet in total. The net in placed in the center of the court, making each side of the net 30 feet by 30 feet. The net is placed directly above the center line, 7 feet 4 inches above the ground for women and 8 feet above the ground for men.


✓ Baseball

✩ How did it start?

↳ Baseball likely had its origins in the early 1800s, possibly as a mash-up of a variety of different stickandball games that had been around for centuries.

✩ What is Baseball ?

↳ a game played by two teams of nine players. Each player from one team hits the baseball and then tries to run around three bases and get to the home base before the other team can get the ball back.

✩ How should you play ?

↳ Teams alternate positions as batters (offense) and fielders (defense), exchanging places when three members of the batting team are “put out.” As batters, players try to hit the ball out of the reach of the fielding team and make a complete circuit around the bases for a “run.” The team that scores the most runs in nine innings (times at bat) wins the game.

✩ Equipment and Facilities

↳ the sport is played with a bat, a ball, and gloves between two teams, each on a field with four white bases laid out in a diamond.


✓ Soccer or Football

✩ How did it start?

↳ soccer records back more than 2,000 years ago to ancient China. Greece, Rome, and parts of Central America also claim to have started the sport; but it was England that transitioned soccer, or what the British and many other people around the world call “football,” into the game we know today.

✩ What is Football ?

↳ football, also called "association football" or soccer, game in which two teams of 11 players, using any part of their bodies except their hands and arms, try to maneuver the ball into the opposing team’s goal.

✩ How should you play ?

↳ The two teams compete to get the ball into the other team's goal. Only the goalkeeper is permitted to handle the ball and may do so only within the penalty area surrounding the goal. The team that scores more goals wins.

✩ Equipment and Facilities

↳ The ball is 68–70 cm (27–28 in) in circumference and known as the football. The structure of a goal varies from sport to sport, and one is placed at or near each end of the playing field for each team to defend.


✓ Rugby Union

✩ How did it start?

↳ Rugby is said to have originated at Rugby School in Warwickshire, England, in 1823 when during a game of football, William Webb Ellis decided to pick up the ball and run with it in his arms.

✩ What is Rugby ?

↳ Also known as rugby football, or simply known as rugby, is a close contact sport that is played with a ball by 2 teams of 15 or 13 players with the objective to ground, pass, or kick the ball behind the opponent's try line, into what is called the in-goal area to score as many points as possible.

✩ How should you play ?

↳ It is a fast-paced team sport. Teams use strength and speed to move the ball down the field. The team on offense tries to pass the ball backward and run toward the goal line while the defensive team tries to stop them by tackling them to the ground. Points are scored by moving the ball past the goal line or kicking it through the uprights, which stand at either end of the field.

✩ Equipment and Facilities

↳ It is typically played on a grass field. The 'in goal' is marked by the "goal line" and the "dead ball line". The length varies between 10 and 22 meters. The goal post comprises two large poles at 5.6 meters wide, with the crossbar being 3 meters above the ground. The ball is the most critical piece of equipment in rugby. It is uniquely shaped unlike any other; an oblong that is completely round and smooth.


✓ Hockey

✩ How did it start?

↳ Hockey started back 4000 years ago with its earliest origins that might be from either Egypt, China, or Persia. The first hockey club int. the world was the "Black Heath Hockey Club" in 1862 before the formation of "Hockey Association" in 1886.

✩ What is Hockey ?

↳ Field hockey is a game that is played on an open field where 2 opposing teams consisting of 6 players each, try to drive a ball into the opponent's net. Ice hockey; similar with field hockey, is played on an ice rink by two opposing teams of 6 skaters who try to knock a flat round rubber disk into the opponent's goal.

✩ How should you play ?

↳ The objective of field hockey is to hit the ball within the striking circle into your opponent's net and score a goal. And for ice hockey, it's to propel a rubber disk called "the puck" past a goal line and into your opponent's net, guarded by a goaltender, or goalie. The team with the most goals at the end of the game wins.

✩ Equipment and Facilities

↳ The hockey stick is a wooden stick with length varying from 26’’ to 38.5’’ depending on height of the player. The bat has a "hooked shape" at the head. The hockey ball is a plastic spherical shaped material made of cork core usually white colored. For ice hockey, the puck made of black vulcanized rubber material. It typically measures about 80 to 85mm in diameter and 25 to 30 mm in thickness and weighs from 1 to 1.5kg. The hockey stick is basically made of wood and graphite and are 150 to 200cm in length with flat horizontal extension known as the "blade".
↳ In ice hockey, players use specialized equipment both to facilitate the play of the game and for protection as this is a sport where injuries are common, therefore, all players are encouraged to protect their bodies from bruises and severe fractures.
