Parts of Speech

✩ What are the "parts of speech"?

↳ They are a class of words wherein a word is assigned to a specific category based on its grammatical functions.

✩ These words are organized into 8 categories which are :


1. Nouns

↳ It is a naming word. It names a person, an animal, a place or a thing. It can also be an abstract concept (something which is not a physical object) such as a feeling or a state.


2. Pronouns

↳ They are the words that are used in place of nouns in sentences. Pronouns refer to either a noun that has already been mentioned, or to a noun that does not need to be named specifically.


3. Verbs

↳ They are words that show an action, wether physical or mental, an occurrence, or state of being.


4. Adverbs

↳ It is a word that describes/modifies a verb, an adjective, another adverb, or even a whole sentence often used to show degree, manner, place, or time.


5. Adjectives

↳ They are words that describe or modify the qualities or states of being of nouns or pronouns.


6. Conjuctions

↳ Are known as connective or linking words. They are parts of speech that connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences together.


7. Preposition

↳ Is a word or group of words used before a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase to show direction, time, place, location, spatial relationships, or to introduce an object and tells you where something is or when something happened.


8. Interjection

↳ Is a short word or phrase that expresses a spontaneous emotion or reaction and often stands alone, unconnected to a sentence.

✩ Why is learning about the parts of speech important ?

↳ Parts of speech are important because they function as the building blocks for grammar.
↳ They show us how different words relate to each other, but also, as soon as we assign each word a role, and put them into a sentence, we actually get something a lot more meaningful.
↳ We would know the role and function that each word has in a sentence structure clearly because it helps us to understand sentences, have the ability to construct them properly, and overall improve in our grammar, comprehension and writing skills.
